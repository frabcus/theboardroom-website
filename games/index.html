What are the games?

Bet on a camel race, build your own zoo, settle a new continent! These are not your usual board games - they're carefully chosen to be fun to play with a clear, dramatic ending. Many of the games are winners of the German "Spiel Des Jahres" (Game of the year) - a highly prestigious award. Below is a description of some of the available games to play.

Camel Up

Winner of the Spiel des Jahres in 2014. In Camel Up, up to 8 players can bet on five racing camels, trying to guess which one will score first place on a track around a pyramid. The earlier you place your be the more you can win. However, camels don't run neatly....sometimes they land on top of one another and hitch a lift across the finish line. Who's going to run when? It all depends on the dice.

Ca$h 'N Gun$

In an abandoned warehouse, a band of gangsters are splitting their latest haul, but they can't come to an agreement on the split. It's time to let the gins do the talking, and soon everyone is aiming at everyone...

All you have to do it stay alive and have the most money at the end of the game! Ca$h 'N Gun$ will make you relive the best scene from gangster movies and other modern detective stories, where the flimsiest excuse serves to let bullets fly! Fun, bluff and negotiations are to be had. Thing is, do you have enough guts to play? This is a game where laughter isn't the only deadly thing....

Ticket to Ride Europe

Spiel des Jahres winner in 2004. This game is easy to learn but difficult to master, Ticket to Ride: Europe is the exciting board game for the whole family! In this brilliant follow up to the original cross country adventure, you must collect cards of various types of train cars, which are then used to claim railway routes in Europe.Each turn you can draw more cards, claim a route, or get additional destination tickets. The winner is the one who scores the most points by completing as many routes as possible. With a detailed board, game cards and train miniatures, Ticket to Ride is an epic journey for 2-5 players across the vast continent of Europe!

Hare and Tortoise

The original Spiel des Jahres winner in 1979! This game is an antique! This is a cunningly designed race to the finish in which your fuel (carrots) must practically run out (10 carrots or fewer) at the moment you cross the finish line. You must also spend your three lettuce cards throughout the duration of the game. The farther you move, the more carrots you spend and their are a variety of ways you can gain or lose carrots as you play.

Carcassonne

Winner of the Spiel des Jahres in 2001. Carcassonne is a clever tile laying game. The southern French city of Carcassonne is famous for its unique Roman and Medieval fortifications. The players develop the area around Carcassonne and deploy their followers on the roads, in their cities and in the fields. The skill of the players to develop the area will determine who is victorious.

Concept

The goal of concept is to guess words through the association of icons. A team of two players - neighbours at the table, chose a word or phrase that the other players need to guess. Acting together, the team places pieces judiciously on the available icons of the game board. To get other to guess "milk" for example, the team might place the pieces on the icons "food/drink", "white" and "liquid". The first player to correctly guess receives two victory points. The winner is the player with the most points at the end of the game.

Bohnanza

Always dreamt of becoming a bean farmer? Who hasn't?! You've got red beans, green beans, black-eyed beans, coffee beans - but where do you plant them? In this game, smart sewing lets you reap rewards. Plant the beans you do want, trade the ones you don't want to other players. Adding to the realism of the game, the player with the most money at the end of the game wins!

Dixit

With 84 beautifully illustrated cards, every picture tells a story, but which story does your picture tell? Each player begins with 6 random playing cards. Each player takes a turn being the story teller and tells a story about one of their images. Each player then pics an image from their hand which matches the story tellers story. Players must then guess which card belongs to the storyteller. This game was awarded the Spiel des Jahres in 2010.
